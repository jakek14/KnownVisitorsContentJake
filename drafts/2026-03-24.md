## Post

Google and Meta keep making it harder to track what happens after the click.

CCPA enforcement just expanded to 19 states. France fined a brand €150M for cookie violations. The EU is coming for everyone.

Marketers are spending hours every week auditing consent banners instead of optimizing campaigns.

Meanwhile the real problem sits untouched — you still don't know who the people on your website actually are.

You can comply with every regulation out there and still be completely blind to 97% of your traffic.

The solution was never better cookies. It's knowing your visitors without them.

## Context

France's CNIL handed SHEIN a €150M fine for cookie consent violations in early 2026. CCPA/CPRA enforcement is now active across 19 US states as of Q1 2026, covering more than half the US population. The regulatory wave is forcing marketers to spend more time on compliance than optimization — while the underlying problem (anonymous website traffic) remains totally unsolved by cookie compliance.

This post anchors on a real, escalating trend (privacy enforcement) to highlight the core KnownVisitors pain point: businesses going blind to their own website visitors as traditional tracking gets regulated away. No product pitch — just a sharp observation about what marketers are missing while they're distracted by compliance theater.
