## Post

Maor Shlomo is 31.

Six months ago he had a side project.

You'd type what you wanted — a CRM, a booking app, a task manager — and it built the thing.

No code. No technical co-founder. No investors.

Grew by word of mouth.

This week, Wix paid him $80 million in cash.

I keep coming back to this because of how many conversations I've had with people who said "I have a great idea but I can't code."

That excuse stopped working about a year ago.

The barrier to building is gone.

What's left is the only part that was ever actually hard — understanding the problem, knowing what to build, moving fast.

Turns out that was always the moat.

## Context

Base44 (built by Maor Shlomo) was acquired by Wix for a reported $80M in cash. The product lets users generate full working applications from natural language prompts — complete with login, data storage, analytics. Built completely solo, bootstrapped, grew via virality.
