## Post

Google changed how it paces ad budgets on March 1.

If you were using ad scheduling as a stealth way to limit monthly spend — weekdays only, specific hours — that no longer works. Google will now push to spend your full monthly limit within whatever hours you pick.

A lot of people are going to notice a surprise overspend this month and have no idea what happened.

This is at least the third time in 18 months that Google has quietly changed how it handles money that isn't theirs. No big announcement. Just a settings update.

The more you build on borrowed infrastructure, the more someone else controls your burn rate.

## Context

On March 1, 2026, Google changed its budget pacing logic for campaigns using ad scheduling. Previously, limiting your schedule (e.g., weekdays only) indirectly capped monthly spend. Now Google proactively tries to spend the full 30.4x daily budget limit within whatever hours the schedule allows — meaning a weekday-only campaign could see monthly spend nearly double overnight if the advertiser doesn't manually adjust daily budgets. Many advertisers used scheduling as a budget guardrail without realizing it, and this change breaks that behavior silently. The post uses this as a launching pad for the broader "platform dependency" theme that ties directly to the case for owning first-party data.
