## Post

OpenAI is charging $60 CPM for ChatGPT ads.

3x the cost of Meta. $200K minimum just to get in the beta.

And the measurement? "High-level views and clicks." That's it.

Every new ad channel does this. Prices start sky-high, attribution is a joke, and marketers pay anyway because they're terrified of missing the wave.

This isn't confidence in the product. It's FOMO pricing.

The channel always wins in year one. The advertiser rarely does.

## Context

On March 2, 2026, Criteo formally announced it had joined OpenAI's advertising pilot, giving ~17,000 advertisers access to ChatGPT ad placements. OpenAI is charging ~$60 CPM (3x typical Meta rates, comparable to Netflix's ad launch pricing in 2022) with a $200K minimum commitment. Early advertisers get only "high-level" performance data — total views and clicks, no granular conversion tracking. This is a sharp hot-take on the new-channel hype cycle that every marketer in the KnownVisitors world will recognize immediately. Pure value/perspective, no KV mention.
