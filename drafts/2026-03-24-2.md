## Post

ChatGPT launched ads this month.

$60 CPM. $200,000 minimum commitment.

Your reporting package: impressions and clicks.

Performance marketers spent a decade building attribution systems so they'd never have to run on vibes again. Now the hottest new channel wants a quarter million upfront and promises to tell you how many people saw the ad.

That's not a new ad platform.

That's a very expensive brand awareness campaign wearing performance marketing clothes.

## Context

ChatGPT entered the advertising market with premium pricing ($60 CPM) and a steep $200K minimum buy-in. Reporting is extremely limited — just impressions and clicks — a significant step backward from the attribution depth marketers expect from Google and Meta. The post challenges the FOMO-driven early adoption narrative by reframing what the product actually is vs. what it's being marketed as. Relevant to KnownVisitors' audience of performance marketers, e-commerce founders, and SaaS operators who live and die by attribution.
