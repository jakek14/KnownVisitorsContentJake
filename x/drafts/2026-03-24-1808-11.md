## Post

ChatGPT just launched ads.

$60 CPM. That's 3x what you pay on Meta.

The pitch is "high intent" — people asking questions before they buy.

But that same person probably already visited your website first.

They were on your pricing page. Comparing you to a competitor. Already close to a decision.

You just didn't know who they were.

So now you'll pay $60 CPM to reach them in a new channel — instead of identifying them when they first showed intent.

Pay to re-acquire someone you already had.

## Context

On March 2, 2026, Criteo announced it joined OpenAI's advertising pilot as the first major ad-tech platform to bring ChatGPT inventory to programmatic buyers. OpenAI is charging ~$60 CPM, roughly 3x Meta CPM rates, with a $200K minimum. The story sparked a lot of discussion about "high-intent" advertising. The angle here flips that conversation — pointing out that the same high-intent user probably already visited your site, and most brands had no way to identify them. Classic problem-awareness post for the KV world.
