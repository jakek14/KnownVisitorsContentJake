## Post

Had coffee with a founder last week.

$40k MRR. Profitable. Growing 15% month-over-month. Customers who actually renew.

He's been fundraising for 4 months. Every meeting ends with "come back with more traction."

Meanwhile, an AI wrapper company down the street raised a $5M seed on a deck and a demo.

Here's what changed: first-time VC funds raised $3.6B last year. Down 85% from $24B in 2021. The money didn't evaporate — it concentrated. Fewer firms. One hot category. One set of rules.

The metrics that got you a seed round in 2021 are what you need for a Series A today — if you're not building something with "AI" in the pitch.

His business isn't broken. His mental model of fundraising is.

Two playbooks exist right now. Pick the one that actually matches what you're building.

## Context

PitchBook/NVCA Venture Monitor data shows first-time VC funds raised just $3.6B in 2025 — an 85% collapse from 2021's $24B peak. The money hasn't disappeared; it's concentrated into a smaller number of established firms almost exclusively chasing AI deals. AI seed-stage startups command a 42% valuation premium over non-AI peers. The bar for non-AI founders has quietly shifted: what used to get a seed round done now barely qualifies for a conversation.
