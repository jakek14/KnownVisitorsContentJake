## Post

The average D2C brand loses $29 acquiring every new customer.

And only 28% of those customers ever come back for a second purchase.

So you paid to get them. You paid to serve them. And then 72% of them just disappeared.

The brands figuring this out aren't buying more traffic. They're getting smarter about the traffic they already have.

Every visitor to your site is a signal. Most brands just throw it away.

That's probably the most expensive thing you're doing that doesn't show up on your P&L.

## Context

New 2026 DTC stats show customer acquisition costs up 222% over 8 years, with the average brand losing $29 per new customer acquired. First-purchase retention dropped to 28%, and true customer loyalty fell from 34% in 2024 to 29% in 2025. The angle here is that the acquisition treadmill is broken — the brands winning are squeezing more value out of existing traffic rather than pouring more into acquisition. Directly relevant to KnownVisitors' world without pitching it.
