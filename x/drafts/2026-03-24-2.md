## Post

The average D2C brand loses $29 acquiring each new customer.

Only 28% of first-time buyers ever come back.

Everyone's obsessed with the first number.

Nobody talks about what happens between the ad click and the sale — the browsers, the almost-buyers, the people who got close and bounced.

You paid to get them to your site.
They left.
You have no idea who they were.

That's not a CAC problem.

That's a visibility problem.

The money's already spent. The gap is in what you do with the traffic you paid for.

## Context

DTC ecommerce stats for 2026 show that customer acquisition costs have exploded — average brand loses $29 per new customer, and repeat purchase rates are abysmal (only 28% ever buy again). The real angle here: everyone talks about lowering CAC or improving LTV, but almost no one talks about the enormous waste in paid traffic that never gets identified or retargeted because those visitors stayed anonymous. This reframe shifts the conversation from "spend less to acquire" to "extract more value from who's already showing up."
