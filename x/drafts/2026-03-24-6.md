## Post

Monday morning. You open Meta Ads Manager. ROAS is down 30%.

Same creative. Same budget. Same offer. Nothing changed on your end.

But Meta quietly redefined what counts as a "click."

As of early March, only actual link clicks qualify for click-through attribution. Social interactions, video engages, all the other stuff that used to count — moved to a different bucket. Conversions didn't disappear. The ruler just changed size.

This is how paid media works. The platform controls the metrics. When they redefine a term, your entire read on what's working shifts overnight — and most people never notice.

Your campaigns didn't fail. You got played by a definition change.

Never let a platform's dashboard be the only thing you trust.

## Context

Meta announced a structural overhaul of its attribution model on March 3, 2026 ("Simplifying Ad Measurement for a Social-First World"). The change moved non-link-click conversions out of standard click-through attribution into a new "engage-through" attribution bucket — causing apparent drops in reported conversions for advertisers who weren't paying close attention. This is a highly relatable pain point for any business owner running paid media, and it reinforces the broader theme of owning your data and not trusting black-box platforms.
