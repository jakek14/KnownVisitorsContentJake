## Post

74% of AI leaders say they're getting more productive.

Only 11% show it on the P&L.

They bought the tools. Did the workshops. Took the screenshots.

"We're more efficient now" — just can't explain where it shows up in the numbers.

## Context

Microsoft Startups' 2026 enterprise trends report surfaced a striking data point: while 74% of AI decision-makers report productivity gains, only 11% can point to measurable financial impact. Two years into mainstream AI adoption and the gap between "we feel faster" and "we're actually making more money" is wide open. The post calls out the AI theater problem in a way that resonates with any founder who's felt pressure to adopt AI tools without a clear ROI story. Short and punchy format with a screenshot-bait last line — challenges the dominant narrative that AI = business results without adding nuance or hedging.
