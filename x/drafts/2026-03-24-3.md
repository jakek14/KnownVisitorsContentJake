## Post

CAC is up 60% since 2023.

The average e-commerce store still converts about 1.4% of visitors.

So you're spending more money to bring 100 people to your site.
98 leave. You never knew who they were.
You just run more ads to replace them.

The whole industry optimizes for the 2%.
Meanwhile the other 98% are just... gone.

## Context

Data-Mania and multiple SaaS/DTC benchmark reports confirm CAC has climbed 40-60% since 2023 due to increased competition, stricter privacy rules, and rising ad costs. Ringly.io's 2026 DTC stats show the average e-commerce store converts 1.4-1.8% of visitors (top 10% hit 4.7%). The combination creates a sharp reframe: the whole industry spends energy optimizing for the tiny sliver that converts, while the vast majority of traffic — real people who showed up with intent — gets ignored entirely. Distinct angle from 2026-03-24-2.md: that post focuses on losing money per customer and the leaky bucket; this one focuses on the industry-wide blind spot of optimizing for the 2% while ignoring the 98%.
