## Post

Shopify's president said AI agents will become personal shoppers.

Not the consumer browsing your site. An AI browsing on their behalf.

The entire e-commerce optimization playbook — popups, cart abandon emails, retargeting pixels, personalized banners — assumes a human is making decisions.

AI agents don't get distracted by a "wait, before you go" offer.

They don't convert on urgency. They don't respond to social proof.

They compare products, evaluate structured data, and transact.

The brands that win the agentic era won't be the ones with the best conversion rate.

They'll be the ones with the cleanest, most accurate product information.

Most people are still optimizing for 2023.

## Context

At the Upfront Summit in LA on March 16, 2026, Shopify president Harley Finkelstein said the company is going "all in on agentic shopping" — AI personal shoppers that browse, compare, and buy on behalf of consumers. He specifically noted that agentic AI will help surface new brands to customers in ways traditional search can't. This is a massive structural shift for e-commerce. The entire CRO, retargeting, and conversion optimization industry was built around persuading humans. If the shopper is an AI, most of that playbook breaks. The post frames this as a thought-leadership take for marketers and e-commerce founders — they need to rethink what optimization even means in the agentic era.
