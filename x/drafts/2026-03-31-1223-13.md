## Post

California just launched a single portal.

One delete request wipes your data from every registered data broker in the state.

Compliance deadline: August 2026. 19 states have privacy laws now. Enforcement just hit all-time highs.

The visitor identification industry is about to get a lot smaller.

When we built KnownVisitors, compliance wasn't a checkbox — it was the whole architecture. Every record scrubbed for CCPA, TCPA, DNC before it ever reaches you.

The tools that treated compliance as an afterthought are going to feel this.

## Context

California's CPPA launched the DROP (DELETE Request and Opt-Out Platform) in 2026, requiring data brokers to process mass consumer deletion requests starting August 2026. CCPA enforcement is at record highs — fines exceeding $1.35M in single actions, 19 states now have comprehensive privacy laws. Most visitor identification tools built their product without compliance infrastructure. KnownVisitors was designed compliance-first. This is a timely, natural moment to contrast that approach without it feeling like a pitch.
