## Post

The most underrated paid channel right now: Reddit.

Most brands are completely ignoring it. Here's what the data says:

Reddit ads are 42% cheaper per click than Facebook.

ROAS is up 82% once you account for the downstream Amazon purchases Reddit discussions drive.

Ad spend on the platform grew 46% last year. Instagram grew 22%. TikTok grew 9%.

The unlock: Reddit users go there specifically to research purchases. They're asking "what's the best X" — and then buying whatever the community recommends.

Ads that match the community tone convert. Polished brand ads don't.

The playbook: pick 3-5 relevant subreddits, write copy that sounds like a real person wrote it, respond to the comments on your ads.

Nobody's competing hard on this yet. That window doesn't stay open long.
