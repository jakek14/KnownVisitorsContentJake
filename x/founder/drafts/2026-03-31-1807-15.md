## Post

**Tweet 1**
The next dollar you earn is probably already a customer.

40% of new ARR at mature SaaS companies comes from expansion — not new logos.

Most founders aren't managing a growth problem. They're managing a retention problem.

**Tweet 2**
Early-stage SaaS loses 6.5% of customers every single month.

That's ~55% gone by year end.

You cannot outrun that number with paid acquisition. You'll spend more replacing those customers than it would have cost to keep them.

**Tweet 3**
And 40% of the churn isn't even the customer's fault.

$1.3B in SaaS revenue is lost every year to failed payments — expired cards, declined transactions.

It's called involuntary churn. It's fixable. Most companies just don't fix it.

**Tweet 4**
The pattern in early-stage SaaS: build, acquire, acquire, acquire.

The pattern in mature SaaS: build, retain, expand, then acquire.

Most founders figure out the difference about two years too late.

**Tweet 5**
If your churn is high and NPS is low, you don't have a marketing problem.

You have a product-market fit problem.

No ad budget fixes that.
