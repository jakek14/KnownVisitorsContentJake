## Post

ChatGPT ads hit $100M ARR in under two months.

OpenAI is projecting $1B in ad revenue for 2026. $25B by 2029.

Anthropic's response: ran a Super Bowl ad explicitly saying Claude will never show ads.

Two different bets on the same moment.

OpenAI is betting people will tolerate ads inside AI the same way they tolerated ads inside search.

Anthropic is betting people are exhausted enough by that experience to pay a premium to avoid it.

Both are probably right — for different users.

The more interesting question is what this does to trust.

Search results spent 20 years getting gradually more polluted with sponsored placements until half the page is now ads.

If the same thing happens to AI answers, the value of having an unbiased AI assistant collapses.

The model that keeps the lights on becomes the model that shapes the recommendations.

We've seen this movie.

## Context

OpenAI's ad pilot launched February 9 and crossed $100M ARR by late March — a pace that would make it one of the fastest ad businesses ever scaled. They're now expanding internationally to Canada, Australia, and New Zealand. Anthropic took the opposite position and used the Super Bowl to say so. This is a genuine industry inflection point: the monetization model for AI is being decided right now, and the outcome will shape how much people trust AI-generated recommendations.
