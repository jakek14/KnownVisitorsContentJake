## Post

70% of SaaS churn is decided in the first 90 days.

Not by your product quality. Not by your pricing.

By whether the customer hit a real moment of value — or didn't.

Companies with time-to-first-value under 7 days show 50% lower churn rates.

That gap is enormous. Almost nobody talks about it.

Everyone obsesses over acquisition. CAC, ROAS, open rates. But the average B2B SaaS company loses 3.5% of customers every month. That's not a sales problem. That's an onboarding problem.

The fastest way to lower CAC? Stop losing the customers you already paid to acquire.

## Context

2026 SaaS churn benchmarks show a clear pattern: the overwhelming majority of churn is determined in the first 90 days, and companies that drive users to a first meaningful outcome within a week retain dramatically better.
