## Post

Vibe coding is the most powerful thing that's happened to indie founders in years.

It's also shipping vulnerabilities at a pace no security team can match.

The AI doesn't know which endpoint is behind your auth wall.
It doesn't know your payment data touches that function.
It doesn't know what "treat this one carefully" means.

You do.

The founders winning with AI-assisted code aren't just the ones moving fastest.

They're the ones who know when to slow down — even when the code "works."

Speed gets you to launch.
Security gets you to scale.

## Context

The vibe coding conversation has shifted in 2026 from "wow, look what I can ship" to "wait, what did I actually ship?" Security researchers and engineering bloggers are documenting real vulnerabilities introduced by AI code generation — particularly around auth logic, data exposure, and dependency misuse. This is a genuine tension for indie founders using Cursor, Claude, Copilot etc. to move fast. The post takes the honest middle ground: AI coding is real and powerful, but founders who succeed long-term are the ones who understand where AI judgment ends and human judgment has to start. Vibe-coding pillar — only used once in recent history and never from this security angle.
