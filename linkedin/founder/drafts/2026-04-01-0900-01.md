## Post

OpenAI launched ads inside ChatGPT on February 9.

$100 million in annualized revenue less than two months later.

Anthropic responded by running a Super Bowl ad that explicitly said: Claude will never show you ads.

Two very different bets.

---

OpenAI's bet: people habituate to ads in AI the same way they habituated to ads in search. The value of "free" outweighs the friction.

Anthropic's bet: people are so burned out on sponsored results cluttering every search page that they'll pay to escape it. Trust is the product.

Both bets could be right. For different users.

---

Here's what concerns me about the OpenAI path:

Search took two decades to get to where it is today — a page where the first four results are ads, the next section is an AI summary with ads, and organic results start halfway down the screen.

The internet has been training us to distrust "results" for years. We developed ad blindness, SEO cynicism, sponsored content fatigue.

If AI recommendations start getting shaped by who paid to be in them, we lose the one thing that made AI assistants feel different: the sense that the answer was actually the best answer.

---

OpenAI says ads will never influence ChatGPT's responses. They said the same about search quality, once.

I hope they're right.
