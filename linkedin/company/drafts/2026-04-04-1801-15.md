## Post

Tariffs just changed the math on every e-commerce marketing budget.

Not next quarter. Now.

Here's what actually happens when your COGS jump 20-30% overnight:

The margin you had on each conversion shrinks by the same amount.

The CAC you could afford last week — you may not be able to afford this week.

That forces a cascade of cuts. And the cuts are predictable.

Top-of-funnel goes first.

Brand awareness campaigns, broad prospecting, high-CPM reach plays — these are the first line items to get slashed when margin pressure hits. The ROI on awareness is hard to defend in a boardroom when the business is bleeding on COGS.

Retargeting survives.

Conversion campaigns targeting warm traffic — people who've already visited, already browsed, already shown intent — keep their budget because the math still works. You're spending money on people who are halfway there.

Cold acquisition gets harder.

With tighter margins, the brands that survive a tariff environment are the ones that have already built a re-engagement infrastructure. The ones that know who showed up, can reach them again, and don't have to pay a platform tax every time to find a new stranger.

This isn't a new playbook.

Every macro cost shock in e-commerce follows the same pattern: when CAC gets expensive, brands shift toward retention and re-engagement. Tariffs are just the newest trigger.

The question isn't whether to shift. It's whether your infrastructure is ready when the shift is forced.

The brands with the cleanest first-party data and the strongest re-engagement workflows will come out of this stronger.

The ones running purely on cold paid acquisition will feel it the most.
