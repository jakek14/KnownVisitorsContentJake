## Post

D2C e-commerce hit $187B last year.

Brands are finally going direct. Cutting out wholesale. Owning the margin. Owning the customer relationship.

The pitch is compelling — and it's working.

But most of them are missing the most obvious piece.

They're sending hard-won traffic to their own site. Traffic they paid for. Traffic they earned.

And 97% of it walks away anonymous.

You can't own the customer relationship if you don't know who your customers are.

Going direct means owning your channel.

It does not automatically mean owning your data.

That's the gap.

At KnownVisitors, we work with D2C brands running beautifully optimized sites with real traffic — who have no idea who's actually visiting.

Going direct is step one.

Knowing who shows up is step two.

Most brands are still on step one.
