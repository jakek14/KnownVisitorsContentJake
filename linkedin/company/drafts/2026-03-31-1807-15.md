## Post

Most visitor identification tools were built for a world that no longer exists.

They work by matching IP addresses to company records. Office IP → company name. Simple.

That playbook made sense in 2019.

Today, over 60% of knowledge workers are remote or hybrid. Nearly half of all B2B website traffic now comes from home offices, coffee shops, and coworking spaces — showing up as residential IPs.

Your visitor ID tool sees that traffic as unidentifiable noise.

It isn't. It's your buyers.

The companies still relying on IP matching are flying blind through most of their funnel — and they don't know it. Their dashboards show "identified visitors." The method is just failing silently.

We built KnownVisitors around identity graphs and email-based matching rather than IP lookup. That's how you get actual people with verified contact info, regardless of where they happen to be working that day.

The old playbook is broken. The data doesn't lie.
