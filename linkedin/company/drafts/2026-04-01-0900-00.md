## Post

The 7 most effective B2B lead generation channels in 2026, ranked by what's actually working — not what's being sold to you.

---

**1. Website visitor identification**
97% of your visitors leave without filling out a form. The companies identifying those visitors before their competitors do are generating pipeline that others can't see, touch, or bid against.

**2. LinkedIn — organic thought leadership + retargeting**
Organic builds trust. Paid retargeting converts it. The accounts doing both in sync are compounding — every post makes the ads work harder.

**3. SEO + GEO-optimized content**
Standard SEO isn't dead, but it's not enough. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) — writing content structured so AI search tools cite it — is now its own discipline. Both matter.

**4. Segmented email to first-party data**
Cold email is dying. Warm email to visitors you've already identified? Different conversation entirely. The list is smaller. The open rate is not.

**5. Google Ads for high-intent search**
Still the most reliable channel for bottom-of-funnel. AI Max is live for all advertisers now — independent data shows mixed results, so test carefully.

**6. Account-based marketing**
ABM finally makes sense when you can identify the individual visitors from your target accounts — not just the company. Otherwise you're throwing personalized content at a ghost.

**7. Educational webinars + ungated content**
B2B buyers in 2026 don't want to fill out a form to read a whitepaper. Ungated content builds trust. Webinars convert trust into conversations. The brands gating everything are watching their reach crater.

---

The thread connecting all seven: first-party data.

Cookies are gone. Third-party lists are degrading. Every channel that still works in 2026 either builds or activates your own data.

The average cost per lead across all channels is $391. The companies extracting the most value from that spend are the ones who know who showed up — not just that someone did.
